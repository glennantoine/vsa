A Lifetime in Aviation

Some people find their calling early in life. For me, it started with building model airplanes as a kid, sparked a fascination that would shape my entire career.

From Navy to Technology Leadership

My journey in aviation began with a decade of service as a Naval Aviation Electronics Technician. Those years weren't just about maintaining complex systems - they were about understanding how technology and aviation intersect. As an Inflight Technician, I logged thousands of hours traversing the globe, experiencing firsthand the critical role that reliable technology plays in aviation operations.

Technology Leadership with an Aviator's Perspective

Today, I bring this comprehensive aviation background to my role as a Fractional CTO. My approach combines deep technical expertise with practical aviation experience. When I work with flight schools, charter services, FBOs, or municipal airports, I'm not just implementing technology - I'm applying decades of aviation insight to solve real operational challenges.

Personal Flying Adventures

Some of my most memorable experiences have been exploring unique airports and destinations across America. Whether it's touching down on Ocracoke Island (W95), where the Cape Hatteras National Seashore provides a stunning backdrop, or navigating to Put-in-Bay (3W2) on South Bass Island in Lake Erie, each flight adds to my understanding of aviation's diverse challenges and opportunities.

I particularly enjoy taking the Aeronca Champ up for low-and-slow flights over central Indiana. These flights remind me why I fell in love with aviation in the first place - the pure joy of flying and the unique perspective it provides.
